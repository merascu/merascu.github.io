Applications for PhD Studies

The Romanian IT sector is currently experiencing a period of uncertainty, as many companies have reduced or relocated their operations. Consequently, some students and professionals are finding it difficult to secure positions in industry and consider pursuing a PhD. Others view doctoral studies as an opportunity for professional retraining, particularly in the field of machine learning.

In recent years, I have been approached by candidates only days before application deadlines, as well as by colleagues seeking a formal supervisor for students who need a PhD to qualify for future academic positions. I have also received inquiries from prospective students who were unable to identify which of my research areas they wished to pursue. Such situations suggest a misunderstanding of what a doctoral program entails, at least in the context of my supervision style and expectations.

(If you are unsure about what a PhD truly involves, feel free to consult ChatGPT or other reputable sources.)

To ensure a productive and mutually beneficial collaboration, I kindly ask prospective PhD candidates to consider the following guidelines:

  • Ideally, we should have worked together previously (e.g., during Bachelor's or Master's studies) or you should come with a strong recommendation from a trusted colleague.
  • Your research interests should align clearly with my ongoing work.
  • I maintain an active and engaged supervision style, which includes regular meetings and continuous feedback.
  • You should be open to constructive criticism.
  • A PhD is a long-term commitment: more a marathon than a sprint.
  • The doctoral thesis should be completed within the time limits set by Romanian regulations (maximum 4 years). Extending the program is not desirable for either of us.

If these expectations resonate with you and your aspirations, I welcome you to reach out for a discussion about potential supervision.

Applications for PostDoc Studies

I only accept post-doctoral candidates whom I have previously worked with, who come recommended by trusted colleagues, and whose research areas align with and can contribute to my own.
